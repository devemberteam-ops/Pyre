# Support

Pyre is free — and not "free trial, card required" free. Free as in *the dev is broke and open-sourced the whole thing.* It's made by one autistic guy, an AI sidekick, and a few randos who lobbed in a tip or a prompt and wandered off. "Ember Team" is a generous word for that. Calibrate your expectations accordingly:

- **We read what we can. A reply is not on the menu.** No SLA, no support desk, no "your call is important to us." If you don't hear back, it isn't personal — it's one broke guy with the same twenty-four hours as everyone else.
- **Other users are your actual support team.** Someone's already hit your problem. Search, ask in the open, answer each other — congratulations, you've invented a community.
- **Bug reports are genuinely welcome** — the single most useful thing you can hand us. Steps, what you expected, what you got. We might not reply; we *do* read them; they make Pyre better. Wild value for free software.
- **It's "as-is."** No warranty, no promises. Bring your own AI key — what your model says is between you, it, and whatever provider you're paying instead of us.

Be decent to each other. That's the entire rulebook.

## Where to take things

- **A question or "how do I…"** → **[Discussions](https://github.com/devemberteam-ops/pyre/discussions)**. Search first — the answer's probably already there.
- **A bug** → **[open an issue](https://github.com/devemberteam-ops/pyre/issues/new/choose)**: steps, what you expected, what you got.
- **Just want the manual** → **[pyrechat.app](https://pyrechat.app)**.
